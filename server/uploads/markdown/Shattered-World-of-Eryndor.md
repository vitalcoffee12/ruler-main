# The Shattered World of Eryndor

## Overview

Eryndor is a fragmented world suspended above a glowing abyss known as **The Luminous Deep**. Thousands of floating landmasses drift slowly through a violet sky, connected by ancient sky-bridges, magical currents, and colossal flying beasts used as living vessels.

Long ago, Eryndor was a single continent. During the **Sundering**, an unknown cosmic force shattered the planet’s crust, lifting entire regions into the air. Strange energies—called **Aetherflux**—now leak from the Deep below, warping nature, empowering magic, and creating creatures that should not exist.

Civilizations cling to floating islands, exploring the sky, excavating ancient ruins, and fighting over dwindling relics from the world before the Sundering.


## The World

### The Luminous Deep

At the center of the sky lies a bottomless glowing ocean of energy.

**Properties**

- Emits Aetherflux radiation that powers magic.
- Creates gravitational anomalies.
- Occasionally births **Aether Storms**, which mutate terrain and creatures.

**Rumors**

- An ancient god sleeps beneath the Deep.
- A lost city floats inside its light.
- Some scholars believe the Deep is the planet’s exposed core.

---

### Aether Currents

Invisible rivers of magical wind flowing between islands.

**Uses**

- Airships navigate them like sea currents.
- Migrating sky creatures follow them.
- Some currents distort time.

**Danger**

- Sudden current shifts can tear ships apart.
- Storm pockets create magical lightning capable of aging metal.

---

## Major Regions

### The Crown Isles

A cluster of golden floating islands orbiting a massive crystal spire.

**Features**

- Capital cities built on marble terraces.
- Largest population center in Eryndor.
- Home to the **Sky Concordat**, a political alliance of noble houses.

**Landmarks**

- **The Helios Spire** – A mile-tall crystal tower regulating Aether currents.
- **The Aurelian Gardens** – Plants glowing with stored sunlight.

---

### The Verdant Drift

A massive jungle island slowly drifting west.

**Characteristics**

- Gigantic trees reaching into cloud layers.
- Rivers that fall off the island’s edges as endless waterfalls.

**Inhabitants**

- Beastfolk tribes
- Sentient plant spirits
- The mysterious **Rootwardens**

**Secrets**

Ancient ruins buried inside tree trunks suggest the forest grew over a forgotten civilization.

---

### The Iron Expanse

A harsh industrial region of metal islands and mining colonies.

**Appearance**

- Black smoke towers
- Aether-powered factories
- Rail systems spanning between floating rocks

**Purpose**

Mining **Aetherite**, a crystal used in weapons and engines.

**Conflict**

Worker revolts against powerful mining guilds are common.

---

### The Whispering Grave

A shattered graveyard of ruins orbiting dangerously close to the Luminous Deep.

**Environment**

- Broken temples
- Floating skeletal structures
- Ghostlike echoes of past civilizations

**Phenomena**

- Time loops
- Disembodied voices
- Phantom armies marching silently

Treasure hunters risk madness exploring the area.

---

## Creatures of Eryndor

### Sky Leviathans

Colossal flying creatures resembling whale-dragons.

**Traits**

- Feed on Aetherflux particles.
- Often mistaken for floating islands.
- Some cultures worship them as gods.

Their bones are used to build nearly indestructible ships.

---

### Lumina Wisps

Small glowing spirits born from Aether storms.

**Behavior**

- Curious and playful
- Can guide travelers through storms
- Occasionally possess machinery

Scholars debate whether they are alive or magical phenomena.

---

### Thorn Behemoths

Massive plant-creatures from the Verdant Drift.

**Abilities**

- Regenerate rapidly
- Control surrounding vines
- Camouflage as forests

They awaken when large areas of jungle are destroyed.

---

## Notable Characters

### Seraphine Vale — Sky Cartographer

Explorer obsessed with mapping the drifting islands.

**Traits**

- Brilliant navigator
- Carries a living compass that points toward unknown lands
- Believes the islands are slowly moving toward a hidden center.

---

### Lord Kael Durn — The Iron Magnate

Ruler of several mining guilds in the Iron Expanse.

**Reputation**

- Ruthless industrialist
- Secretly searching for an artifact called the **World Anchor**

If activated, it may reunite the floating continents.

---

### The Rootwarden

An ancient being merged with the heart of the Verdant Drift.

**Nature**

- Neither human nor plant
- Speaks through trees
- Protects the balance of nature

Legends claim the Rootwarden remembers the world before the Sundering.

---

## Artifacts & Items

### Aetherite Crystals

The most valuable resource in Eryndor.

**Uses**

- Power airships
- Amplify magic
- Fuel advanced weapons

Overexposure causes **Aether Sickness**.

---

### Compass of Unwritten Skies

A legendary navigation device.

**Ability**

Points toward locations that **do not yet exist on any map**.

Some believe it can lead to entirely new floating continents.

---

### The World Anchor

A mythical relic capable of stabilizing islands.

**Potential Effects**

- Stop island drifting
- Reconnect landmasses
- Or collapse the entire sky-world.

No one knows where it is hidden.

---

## Factions

### The Sky Concordat

A coalition of noble houses controlling trade routes.

**Goal**

Maintain political stability between floating nations.

---

### The Aether Prospectors Guild

Industrial faction mining Aetherite.

**Goal**

Control energy resources across the sky.

---

### The Rootwardens

Mystical protectors of nature.

**Goal**

Prevent civilization from draining the world’s magical core.

---

### The Veil Seekers

Secretive explorers searching for lost pre-Sundering knowledge.

**Belief**

The Sundering was not an accident.

---

## Adventure Hooks

### The Moving Island

A previously stable island suddenly begins drifting rapidly toward the Luminous Deep.

Something beneath its surface is awakening.

---

### Leviathan Graveyard

A newly discovered cloud region filled with the skeletons of Sky Leviathans.

Inside one skull lies an ancient city.

---

### The Returning Continent

Explorers detect a **massive landmass emerging from the Luminous Deep**.

It may be a lost part of the original world.

Or something far older.

---

## Tone of the World

- Exploration and discovery
- Political intrigue between sky nations
- Ancient mysteries buried in drifting ruins
- A balance between nature, industry, and magic

Eryndor is a world constantly moving — literally and politically — where maps become obsolete within years and every horizon hides a new island.
